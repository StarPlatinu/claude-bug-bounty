🚀 Quick Start (5 minutes)
1
Add your target
Type a domain in the sidebar (e.g. hackerone.com) and press Enter or click +.
2
Run recon
Click /recon in the quick bar. Discovers subdomains, live hosts, URLs, and runs nuclei automatically.
3
Hunt for bugs
Click /hunt. Auto-selects vuln classes from the tech stack, or pick one from the dropdown (ssrf, idor, xss…).
4
Validate the finding
Click /validate. Walks you through the 7-Question Gate. Score ≥ 7 = submit, 5–6 = chain first, < 5 = move on.
5
Generate report
Click /report and pick your platform. Creates H1 / Bugcrowd / Intigriti Markdown + JSON.
🎯 The 7-Question Gate

Runs before every report. Kills weak findings before they hurt your validity ratio.

Weight

💡 Pro Tips
🔗
Chain low-severity bugs — a Medium SSRF + IDOR can become a Critical. Run /chain before giving up on a finding.
⚡
Use autopilot on new targets — /autopilot target.com --mode normal does the full loop while you review at each step.
📖
Run /intel first — knowing what CVEs and past disclosures exist tells you where to focus before spending time on recon.
🚫
Never-submit list — self-XSS, missing security headers, rate-limit on non-critical endpoints, clickjacking on static pages. These are always rejected.
📊
Check the Stats tab after each hunt — the severity bar and nuclei findings list shows you the most promising attack surface at a glance.
📋
Export findings as CSV — paste directly into your notes or share with a team member from the Findings tab.
🎯
5-minute rule — if you're getting nothing on an endpoint after 5 minutes, move on. Use /surface to find the next best place to look.
